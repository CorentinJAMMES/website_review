Review: Forge of Empires – Building History, One Era at a Time

Posted on February 10, 2026 by Thomas Dequirez
Strategy Management Multiplayer

Forge of Empires is a browser-based strategy game developed by InnoGames, set in a historical and fantasy world where players build and expand their own city from the Stone Age to the modern era and beyond. Players research technologies, construct buildings, manage resources, and engage in diplomacy or warfare with neighbors. The game features turn-based combat, a single-player campaign, and multiplayer interactions, including guilds and player-versus-player battles. Its blend of city-building, strategy, and historical progression has made it popular among fans of long-term, tactical gameplay.

A World Shaped by Time

Unlike most games that focus on a single era or real-time battles, it lets players evolve their civilization from the Stone Age to the future, introducing new technologies, units, and buildings with each era. Its single-player campaign adds depth, while guilds and diplomacy create a social, cooperative layer. The game avoids the "pay-to-win" pitfall, making it accessible and rewarding for strategic players.

The game’s pacing is masterful. Unlike other strategy games that overwhelm you with tasks, Forge of Empires balances progression and challenge. The research tree is deep but intuitive, and the quest system guides new players without holding their hand.

The Verdict

With features like the Guild Expeditions (where you team up with others to conquer challenging maps) and the Great Map (a persistent world where players compete for territories), Forge of Empires offers endless content. It’s a game that rewards patience, strategy, and teamwork—making it a timeless classic in the strategy genre.
