Review : Pokémon Black 2 and White 2!

Posted on February 8, 2026 by Thomas Dequirez
RPG Nintendo DS Strategy

When Game Freak announced Pokémon Black 2 and White 2, fans were skeptical about a direct sequel. Yet, it became a masterpiece. For me, this review is special. This was one of the very first video games I ever played. Despite the passage of time and new console generations, it remains my absolute favorite game, holding a nostalgic power that few other titles can match.

A Region Frozen in Fear

The story takes place two years after the original games. The narrative is darker and more mature. The villainous group, Neo Team Plasma, has returned. Unlike their previous iteration where they pretended to care about Pokémon liberation, they are now full-blown terrorists.

Led by the cold, calculating scientist Colress(nikolaï in french) and the maniacal mastermind Ghetsis(Ghetis in french), their goal is simple: world domination using the power of the legendary dragon Kyurem to freeze the Unova region. The stakes feel genuinely high, and the story pacing is excellent.

Technical Performance & Polish

In an era where modern games often release with glitches, Black 2 and White 2 stand as a testament to quality. "Are there any bugs?" you might ask. The answer is: very few.

The game is incredibly polished. Aside from some minor graphical glitches involving the "Zoroark" ability in very specific setups, the experience is seamless. The sprite work is the peak of the 2D era, fluid and full of life, free from the performance issues that plague later 3D entries.

The Verdict

With the addition of the Pokémon World Tournament (where you can fight past gym leaders) and the "Memory Link" feature that connects to your save file from the first game, B2W2 offers endless content. It is a perfect farewell to the DS era.

Verdict

10/10

"A Timeless Classic"

Defined by a strong narrative, iconic villains like Ghetsis, and flawless performance. It's a must-play.

Did you know?
This is the only generation in Pokémon history to receive a direct numbered sequel (2) instead of a third version (like Emerald or Platinum).
